AI in SEO: Adapt to AI Overviews, Tools & the Zero-Click Future

AI is reshaping every layer of search — from how Google ranks content to how users get answers. Here's how to stay ahead of every shift affecting Indian fintech SEO.

How AI Is Changing Search

Artificial intelligence has fundamentally altered how Google processes queries, how results are generated, and how users interact with search. The key AI systems reshaping SEO in 2026: BERT and MUM (understanding natural language query intent), RankBrain (interpreting ambiguous queries), AI Overviews (synthesizing answers from multiple sources), and Gemini (Google's foundation model powering increasingly sophisticated search experiences).

For Indian fintech sites, the most immediate AI-driven change is the rise of AI Overviews for informational queries. A user asking "how to improve CIBIL score" now often sees a synthesized answer before any links — and that answer may draw from multiple sources, not all of which are in the top 10 organic results. This is simultaneously a threat (reduced CTR for informational content) and an opportunity (if your content is the source being cited in the AI Overview, your brand gets enormous visibility at position zero).

AI Overviews — Threat or Opportunity

AI Overviews reduce click-through rates for informational queries by giving users the answer without requiring a click. Research suggests clicks on informational queries drop by 20–60% when an AI Overview appears. This is a genuine threat to content-heavy fintech sites that rely on informational traffic for top-of-funnel visibility. The question is not whether AI Overviews affect you, but how to adapt.

The adaptation strategy: shift content focus toward queries where AI Overviews are less likely to appear (highly specific, transactional, commercial queries), optimize informational content to be cited in AI Overviews (making your brand visible even without the click), and use informational content as a brand awareness channel rather than a pure traffic channel. BankBazaar's strategy of targeting "how to calculate home loan EMI" captures brand recognition in AI Overviews even when users don't click through — because the AI Overview says "according to BankBazaar..."

Using AI Tools in Your SEO Workflow

AI tools have transformed the productivity of SEO practitioners. Used correctly, they compress weeks of work into hours. The key is understanding what AI tools excel at and where human judgment remains essential.

Protecting Your Content in the AI Era

AI web crawlers from OpenAI (GPTBot), Anthropic (ClaudeBot), Google (GoogleBot-Extended), Perplexity (PerplexityBot), and others are continuously crawling the web to train AI models and generate real-time responses. As a website owner, you have a choice: allow, restrict, or selectively permit these crawlers via your robots.txt file.

The strategic decision is nuanced. Blocking all AI crawlers (Disallow: /) prevents your content from being used in AI training — which might protect against content duplication but also prevents you from being cited in AI responses, reducing your GEO visibility. The recommended approach for most fintech sites: allow legitimate crawlers with good track records (Perplexity, Google AI), selectively block those you're less certain about, and explicitly disallow known scraping bots that don't provide attribution.

1
Audit Your Current robots.txt for AI Crawlers
Check your robots.txt for GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and GoogleBot-Extended rules. If you have none, decide your policy: allowing citation-giving AI crawlers while blocking pure scrapers is a reasonable starting position.
2
Implement AI Crawler Rules
Use the Robots.txt Generator to create a robots.txt with specific AI crawler rules. For GEO visibility: allow GPTBot and PerplexityBot. For content protection on proprietary research: block GPTBot from your /research/ section while allowing access elsewhere.
3
Optimize for AI Overview Citations
Identify queries where AI Overviews appear (you can see this in Google Search). For each, check if your content is being cited. If not, apply AEO principles (Lesson 11): direct answers, structured content, FAQ schema, high E-E-A-T signals.
4
Build the Zero-Click Brand Strategy
Accept that AI Overviews will reduce clicks on some queries. Optimize for brand visibility within AI Overviews rather than clicks. Include your brand name in your content's key summary sentences so when AI cites you, it says "According to [Brand]" rather than just pulling anonymous information.

The Zero-Click Future

The "zero-click search" phenomenon — where users get their answer in the SERP without visiting any website — has been growing for years and is accelerating with AI Overviews. For informational queries, zero-click rates in India are already estimated at 65%+ in some categories. This is not a temporary trend — it is the direction of search's evolution.

The appropriate response is not panic but strategic adaptation: double down on content types that are inherently click-generating (tools, interactive content, in-depth guides that require reading the full piece, transactional pages where users need to fill a form), build email and notification lists to reduce search dependency, optimize for AI citation brand visibility, and invest in E-E-A-T signals that make your brand the trusted authority AI systems consistently cite.

📌 Key Takeaways
  • AI Overviews reduce clicks on informational queries — the strategic response is optimizing to be cited within them (brand visibility) while shifting content investment toward transactional queries.
  • AI tools dramatically accelerate SEO work but require human oversight — especially for YMYL financial content where AI errors cause real harm.
  • Manage AI crawlers deliberately in robots.txt — allow citation-giving AI systems (Perplexity, Google AI) to boost GEO visibility, block pure scrapers that don't attribute sources.
  • Zero-click search is accelerating — diversify traffic sources, build owned audiences (email, notifications), and invest in transactional content that AI Overviews can't fully satisfy.
  • The SEO practitioners who thrive in the AI era use AI tools for efficiency but maintain the human judgment, subject expertise, and ethical standards that AI cannot replace.
