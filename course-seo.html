Search Engine Optimization

Rank higher, earn more organic traffic, and survive Google's AI era — a practitioner's guide to modern SEO.

⏱ 3 min read ✍️ Lochan Yadav · BankBazaar 📅 May 2026

What Is SEO?

SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of making your website appear higher in search engine results pages (SERPs) — without paying for ads. When someone searches "best credit card in India," the sites that show up on page one didn't get there by accident. They earned those positions through content, authority, and technical signals that Google's algorithm rewards.

Simple definition: SEO = making your website easy for Google to find, understand, and trust — so it ranks your pages above your competitors.
🏗️

The 3 Pillars of SEO
On-Page (Content & Keywords) · Off-Page (Links & Authority) · Technical (Speed, Structure, Crawlability)

Real-World Example

At BankBazaar, we optimized our personal loan pages for queries like "personal loan interest rates 2026." By updating the content with current rates, adding FAQ schema, and improving page speed, organic traffic to those pages grew by 38% in 90 days — with zero ad spend.

The 3 Pillars of SEO

1. On-Page SEO

On-page SEO is everything you control on your own page. This includes your title tag (the clickable headline in search results), your meta description, your headings (H1, H2, H3), the body content, internal links, image alt text, and structured data (schema markup). The goal is to make your page the most relevant and helpful answer for a specific search query.

2. Off-Page SEO

Off-page SEO is about building authority. The most important signal is backlinks — other websites linking to yours. A link from The Times of India to BankBazaar tells Google "this is a trustworthy source." Quality beats quantity: one link from a high-authority financial site is worth more than 50 links from random blogs.

3. Technical SEO

Technical SEO ensures Google can find, crawl, and index your site efficiently. Key areas: site speed (LCP under 2.5s), mobile friendliness, Core Web Vitals, HTTPS, XML sitemaps, and a clean robots.txt. A great piece of content that can't be crawled won't rank.

Step-by-Step SEO Process

1

Keyword Research

Find what your audience searches for. Use Ahrefs or Google Keyword Planner to identify high-volume, low-competition queries relevant to your business.

2

Map Keywords to Pages

Assign one primary keyword per page. Avoid cannibalization — don't have two pages fighting for the same query.

3

Create / Optimize Content

Write content that fully answers the search intent. Cover the topic better than anyone on page one — more depth, fresher data, clearer structure.

4

On-Page Optimization

Place your keyword in the title tag, first 100 words, at least one H2, and the meta description. Add schema markup for FAQs or articles.

5

Build Backlinks

Use digital PR, guest posts, and broken link building to earn links from relevant, authoritative domains in your industry.

6

Monitor & Iterate

Track rankings weekly using Ahrefs or Google Search Console. Update content every 3-6 months. SEO is never "done."

SEO in 2026: The AI Layer

Google's AI Overviews now appear for 70%+ of informational queries. To appear in these AI-generated answers, your content must be factual, structured, and citeable. Use clear headings, FAQ sections, tables, and statistics with sources. This is AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — the next evolution of SEO.

Pro tip from BankBazaar: Pages with FAQ schema that directly answer specific questions (e.g., "What is the SBI home loan interest rate?") are 3× more likely to be cited in AI Overviews than generic long-form articles.
