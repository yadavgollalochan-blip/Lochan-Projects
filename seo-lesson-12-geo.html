GEO: Generative Engine Optimization — Get Cited by AI Systems

As ChatGPT, Perplexity, and AI Overviews become primary information channels, being cited by AI is the new organic ranking — here's how to earn those citations.

What Is GEO

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the emerging practice of optimizing your content and brand to be cited, referenced, and recommended by AI systems — including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google's AI Overviews. As AI-powered search and chat interfaces become primary information discovery channels, being cited by these systems is the next frontier of organic visibility.

GEO is distinct from AEO (which targets Google's featured snippets) and traditional SEO (which targets link rankings). AI systems don't rank pages — they synthesize information and cite sources they deem authoritative, accurate, and well-structured. A brand that appears in 60% of AI responses to questions about mutual fund investing in India has fundamentally different market visibility than one that ranks #1 on Google for three keywords. GEO is about becoming part of the AI's "knowledge base" for your domain. Read the full GEO guide for comprehensive coverage.

How AI Systems Choose What to Cite

AI language models are trained on large corpora of web content, academic papers, and curated datasets. When they generate responses, they tend to cite sources that appear frequently in their training data, have high authority signals (links, brand mentions, official status), are well-structured and clearly written, contain original, specific, and accurate information, and use natural language that matches the conversational style of the query.

For Indian financial content, AI systems have learned from millions of articles, discussions, and official documents about Indian finance. The brands cited most frequently by AI for investment queries are those whose content appeared most in high-quality contexts: Zerodha (because Varsity has millions of citations and is genuinely the best free investment education in India), Moneycontrol (scale of coverage), RBI and SEBI official sites (authoritative), and Economic Times (reach and credibility). To become a cited source, you need to appear repeatedly in contexts that AI training associates with quality and authority.

The 5 GEO Optimization Principles

Based on research by Columbia University's GEO team and practical experimentation, five content qualities significantly increase the probability of being cited by AI systems: statistics and data, cited sources within your content, fluent and quotable writing, breadth of coverage on the topic, and uniqueness of information presented.

1
Include Original Statistics and Data
AI systems strongly prefer citing content with specific, verifiable numbers. Instead of "many Indians invest in mutual funds," write "As of 2026, over 5.2 crore unique investors hold mutual fund folios in India (AMFI data)." Original proprietary data (your own research, surveys) is even more valuable — it makes you the primary source that others cite.
2
Cite Authoritative Sources Within Your Content
AI systems learn citation patterns from human writing. Content that itself cites RBI, SEBI, AMFI, and academic research teaches AI that your content operates at the same quality level. Add outbound citations to official sources the way an academic paper does — with context ("According to RBI's 2026 Financial Stability Report...").
3
Write in Clear, Quotable Sentences
AI systems pull specific sentences and phrases when generating responses. Write in clear, self-contained statements that make sense out of context: "The minimum SIP investment in most Indian mutual funds is ₹100 per month." This sentence, standing alone, is quotable. Vague or hedged writing never gets cited.
4
Cover Topics with Comprehensive Breadth
AI systems are more likely to cite comprehensive sources that cover the full spectrum of a topic rather than thin articles that only cover one angle. A Zerodha Varsity module on equity investing covers fundamentals, technical analysis, derivatives, and risk management — AI recognizes it as a comprehensive resource and cites it across multiple related queries.
5
Present Unique, Non-Obvious Information
AI systems are particularly likely to cite content that provides information not widely available elsewhere. Original research findings, proprietary data analyses, unique case studies, and expert insights that can't be found on Wikipedia or generic finance sites stand out as citation-worthy.

GEO for Indian Financial Brands

For Indian fintech brands, GEO is a massive untapped opportunity. Most current AI citations in the Indian financial space go to Zerodha's Varsity, Moneycontrol, Economic Times, and official government sites — primarily because of their scale, depth, and historical web presence. This means there's significant room for challenger brands with genuinely excellent content to break into AI citation patterns.

Practical GEO actions for Indian fintech: create comprehensive, citation-ready guides on topics where AI is currently underserved (e.g., NRI investment regulations in India, detailed state-specific GST filing guides, sector-specific equity analysis); establish your brand as a primary source for original research (quarterly investment behavior surveys, credit score trend reports); and ensure your Hreflang and schema markup make your content accessible to AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot).

Zerodha GEO — Why AI Always Cites Varsity

Zerodha's Varsity platform is the most-cited Indian investment education resource by AI systems. The reasons: (1) 60+ comprehensive modules with hundreds of clearly structured lessons — AI recognizes breadth and depth. (2) Content written in first-person by practitioners with real trading experience — AI values experiential authority. (3) Open licensing that allows Varsity content to appear in many secondary contexts — amplifying its web presence. (4) Consistent factual accuracy with regular updates — AI systems prefer content that remains accurate over time. Zerodha didn't optimize Varsity for AI — they created genuinely excellent content, and AI recognition followed as a consequence.

Measuring GEO Performance

GEO measurement is less mature than traditional SEO analytics — there's no "GEO Search Console" yet. Current measurement approaches: manually querying 20–30 target questions in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews monthly and recording which sources are cited; tracking brand mentions using tools like Mention or Brandwatch for AI-generated content; and monitoring referral traffic from Perplexity (which sends measurable referral traffic to cited sources, identifiable in GA4 as referral traffic from perplexity.ai).

Tools specifically being built for GEO measurement include BrightEdge's Share of Voice for AI, Semrush's AI Overview tracker, and various emerging GEO-specific analytics platforms. Set up Perplexity.ai referral tracking in GA4 immediately — it's currently one of the cleanest AI referral signals measurable in standard analytics.

📌 Key Takeaways
  • GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is about getting cited by AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini — the next frontier of organic visibility.
  • AI systems prefer citing content with original data/statistics, authoritative outbound citations, comprehensive topic coverage, and clear quotable writing.
  • Zerodha Varsity is the benchmark for Indian financial GEO — comprehensive, practitioner-written, accurate, and freely accessible to AI crawlers.
  • Measure GEO by manually testing target queries in major AI systems monthly and tracking Perplexity referral traffic in GA4.
  • Allow legitimate AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot) in your robots.txt — blocking them prevents your content from being included in future AI training and responses.
