Search Engine Marketing

Pay-per-click ads, Smart Bidding, Quality Score, and ROAS — everything you need to run Google Ads that actually convert.

⏱ 3 min read ✍️ Lochan Yadav · BankBazaar 📅 May 2026

What Is SEM?

SEM (Search Engine Marketing) is the practice of buying visibility in search engine results through paid advertising. While SEO earns traffic organically over time, SEM buys it immediately. The dominant platform is Google Ads (formerly AdWords), which serves text ads, shopping ads, and display ads across Google Search and its partner network.

Key difference: SEO = earned rankings that take months to build. SEM = bought rankings that go live in hours. The best digital marketers use both together.
💡

How SEM Works
You bid on keywords → Google shows your ad to searchers → You pay only when someone clicks → They land on your page → They (hopefully) convert

Real-World Example

A fintech brand running Google Ads for "home loan calculator" bids ₹45 per click. With a 4% conversion rate and an average loan value of ₹50 lakh, each conversion is worth thousands in commission — making the ₹45 CPC extremely profitable. SEM works best when the math checks out.

Key SEM Concepts

Quality Score

Google rates your ads on a 1-10 scale called Quality Score. It's based on expected CTR, ad relevance, and landing page experience. A higher Quality Score means you pay less per click than competitors with the same bid. It's Google's way of rewarding relevance.

Bidding Strategies

Manual CPC gives you full control. Smart Bidding (Target CPA, Target ROAS, Maximize Conversions) uses Google's AI to optimize bids in real-time using hundreds of signals including device, location, time of day, and audience. In 2026, Smart Bidding outperforms manual in most scenarios — but only after enough conversion data.

Ad Extensions (Now "Assets")

Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, call buttons, and image assets expand your ad's real estate on the SERP. They're free to add and significantly improve CTR. Always use all relevant extensions.

Step-by-Step SEM Campaign Setup

1

Define Your Goal

Leads? Sales? App downloads? Your goal determines your campaign type and bidding strategy. Set this before touching the platform.

2

Keyword Research

Use Google Keyword Planner or Ahrefs to find high-intent keywords. Focus on transactional terms ("buy," "apply," "best," "near me") that signal purchase intent.

3

Structure Your Campaign

One campaign = one goal. One ad group = one tight keyword theme. This maximises Quality Score and makes reporting clean.

4

Write Compelling Ad Copy

Lead with the benefit, include the keyword, add a clear CTA. Use responsive search ads with 15 headlines and 4 descriptions — let Google test combinations.

5

Build a Dedicated Landing Page

Never send paid traffic to your homepage. Match the ad's promise exactly. One page, one offer, one CTA. Fast load time is non-negotiable.

6

Set Up Conversion Tracking

Install Google Tag via Google Tag Manager. Track form fills, calls, and purchases. Without this, Smart Bidding has nothing to optimize.

7

Monitor, Optimize, Scale

Review search term reports weekly to add negatives. Pause underperforming ads. Scale budgets to winning campaigns. Check ROAS against your target.

Negative keywords are your best friend: If you're selling premium home loans, add "free," "cheap," and "calculator only" as negatives. Irrelevant clicks are wasted budget.
